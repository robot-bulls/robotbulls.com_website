The Abraham Accords: Prosperity Through Peace
View Panel
Sir Martin Sorrell
Founder and Executive Chairman

Sir Martin Sorrell delves into the transformative impact of the Abraham Accords on regional economies, businesses, and peacebuilding. This groundbreaking agreement has sparked economic growth, innovation, and collaboration across the Middle East, setting the stage for a brighter, more prosperous future.

Key Highlights:

- Trade between Israel and the UAE reached $2.5 billion in 2022, with projections of $1 trillion in economic activity for the region over the next decade.
- 500,000 new jobs expected in technology and innovation sectors by 2030.
- Tourism boom: Over 1 million Israeli tourists visited the UAE in two years, fostering cultural and economic ties.
Renewable energy projects could save the region $15 billion in energy costs by 2030.
This session showcases how diplomacy can drive economic and social progress, transforming the region and setting a global example for prosperity through peace. Don’t miss this chance to hear from one of the world’s top business leaders.

Cinema as a Catalyst for Human Fraternity
View Panel
Taha Shah Badussha
Actor | Trailblazer in Indian Cinema

Cinema has the unparalleled power to shed light on untold stories and spark movements for human fraternity. This impactful 30-minute session brings together renowned filmmakers, actors, and legal experts to explore how films inspire empathy, understanding, and justice in the face of historical and modern challenges:

Alain Williams, director of the upcoming film "THE HERITAge" starring John Malkovich, delves into the theft of Jewish art during World War II, a powerful true story that highlights the ongoing fight to return stolen cultural heritage to its rightful owners.

Taha Shah Badussha, acclaimed Bollywood actor, discusses Paro: The Untold Story of Slavery, a bold and evocative film exposing the plight of brides sold into marriage, igniting global conversations about human rights and social justice.

Prominent French Lawyer Jean-Luc Chetboun, a leading voice in the battle against modern-day art spoilage, shares insights on how legal frameworks and awareness campaigns are essential to preserving cultural heritage.

Key Takeaways:

- Discover how films can serve as a lens to uncover hidden histories and promote justice and unity.
- Understand the intersection of art, law, and cinema in preserving cultural identity and human rights.
- Gain insights from leading voices on how creative storytelling influences collective awareness and action for a more united future.
Join us to celebrate cinema’s ability to inspire change and advance human fraternity in an increasingly divided world.
